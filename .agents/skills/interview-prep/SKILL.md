# Prepare for an interview

1. Use only the opportunity ID supplied by Scout. Never switch to another tracker entry.
2. Read `workspace.json`, `profile/context.md`, `profile/calibration.md`, `cv/master-cv.md`, the selected tracker entry, its tailored CV/evidence, its general job chat, and its company timeline/contact file when present.
3. If stage, format, interviewer, or timing details would materially improve the work and are missing, ask one focused question at a time. Before a stage is recorded, label predictions as general preparation.
4. Re-check the role source and current public company sources. Prefer official pages and filings, then reputable reporting. Cite each public fact with its URL and the absolute date checked; distinguish fact, inference, and unknown.
5. Write or refresh the exact prep-pack path supplied by Scout. Keep it specific to this opportunity.

Include:

- interview snapshot and known logistics;
- company, product, business-model, customer, market, and recent-development briefing;
- role and stage expectations;
- likely questions with evidence-backed answer outlines;
- reusable STAR stories drawn only from confirmed evidence;
- honest gaps, difficult questions, and safe ways to address them;
- thoughtful questions for the interviewer aligned with the user's priorities;
- interviewer and relationship context from private records;
- concise opening pitch, closing points, and practical checklist;
- sources with URLs and checked dates;
- a `## My notes` section owned by the user.

When refreshing, preserve `## My notes` exactly. Do not replace an existing pack if live research fails; explain the limitation in chat. If no pack exists, a clearly labelled local-evidence-only draft is allowed.

For practice, ask one question at a time, wait for the answer, then give concise feedback on relevance, evidence, clarity, and unsupported claims. Offer follow-up questions without fabricating an employer process.

Never invent experience, metrics, contacts, company facts, or role facts. Never send messages, apply, change tracker status, modify another opportunity, or commit. The Scout runtime handles persistence and backup.
